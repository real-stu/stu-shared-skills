# Direct-Response Craft (Long-Form & Universal)

Sentence-level persuasion craft for anything longer than an ad: sales pages, VSLs, emails, advertorials — plus universal headline/opening/flow techniques that improve every format. Pairs with meta-direct-response.md (Meta specifics) and landing-page-copy.md (bridge pages).

Adapted from Rob Palmer's direct-response-copy skill (robpalmer.com, CC-BY-4.0).

Core principle: write like you're explaining to a smart, skeptical friend. Back every claim with specifics. Make the transformation viscerally clear. The reader shouldn't notice the technique.

## Headlines (80% of the work; one can outpull another 19.5x — test 5+)

- **Master formula:** [action verb] + [specific outcome] + [timeframe or contrast] — "Ship your startup in days, not weeks"
- **Story:** "They laughed when I sat down at the piano... but when I started to play!" — pattern: "They [doubted] when I [action]... but when I [result]"
- **Specificity (Ogilvy):** "[specific metric] + [unexpected detail]" — reader self-persuades ("At 60 mph the loudest noise is the electric clock")
- **Question:** "Do you make these mistakes in English?" — self-selecting
- **Transformation:** "From [bad state] to [good state]"
- Failures: clever over clear, no self-interest, vague, no curiosity gap

## Opening Lines (job: get sentence two read)

Direct challenge ("You've been using X wrong"), story in-scene ("Last Tuesday I opened my laptop and saw $47,329 in one day"), confession ("I almost gave up three times"), specific result, matching question, or Sugarman's short sentence ("It's simple."). Never: "In today's fast-paced world...", "Are you ready to...", "In this article you'll learn..."

## Open Loops & Flow (the Slippery Slide)

- Create a gap: "I tested 47 headlines. One pattern beat everything by 3x." (which?)
- Seeds of curiosity ending paragraphs: "But that's not even the best part." — 2-4 per page max
- Partial reveal: "Three parts. The first is obvious. The third is counterintuitive. But the second? That's where the magic happens."
- CLOSE every loop you open — small ones within 1-3 paragraphs, big ones by the end
- Bucket brigades ("Here's the thing:", "Turns out", "The result?"); stutter technique (repeat a word across the paragraph break); stupidly-easy short first sentences; vary paragraph length for rhythm
- Momentum killers: jargon, unbroken paragraphs, tangents, repeated sentence structure

## Pain Quantification

Do the math for them: "4 hrs emails + 6 hrs landing page + 4 hrs webhooks + 2 hrs SEO = 22+ hours of headaches. There's an easier way." Or the scenario they've lived. Quantified problems feel solvable and priceable.

## Rhythm: Alternation

Where AI copy fails most. Short sentence. Impact. Then a longer one that breathes and adds context. Pattern: hook (short) → expand (breathe) → land it (kicker). Repeat.

## Trust Elements

- **Founder story** (highest-trust element): humble origins → struggle → discovery → success → offer; vulnerability → credibility → shared journey
- **Testimonials as mini case studies:** [before] + [action] + [specific outcome] + [timeframe] + [emotional reaction] — "I shipped in 6 days as a noob coder. I wanna cry 🥲"
- **Disqualification (velvet rope):** "You're a fit if... / NOT a fit if..." — flips please-buy into prove-you're-worthy, pre-filters complainers
- **CTAs describe the benefit,** don't command: "See the exact template I used" not "Learn More". Below the button: [risk reversal] + [social proof] + [speed/ease] — "$199 once. Join 2,600+ marketers. 2 minutes to install."

## Full Landing/Sales Page Sequence

Hook (outcome headline w/ number) → problem (quantified) → agitate (vivid scenario) → credibility (founder story/proof) → solution (as transformation) → proof (specific testimonials) → objections (FAQ or fit/not-fit) → offer (price + value justification) → urgency (only if authentic) → final CTA (benefit + friction reducers).

## Video Scripts: Format for the Editor

Deliver the **voiceover as the primary artifact** with minimal directions. Editors read brackets as mandatory — over-directed scripts trigger alignment meetings; sparse scripts ship. Add a bracketed cue ONLY when: a number/claim/URL must appear on-screen; a specific asset must hit at a precise moment; a beat depends on visual timing; or the B-roll is counterintuitive. Format: plain-text VO paragraphs + one-line cues like `[ON-SCREEN: "$47/month"]`. If asked for a "full shot list", confirm first — usually the lean VO script is what's wanted.

## The Read-Aloud Test

Before shipping: sounds like talking? Would I say it to a friend? Every claim has a number/proof? Rhythm alternates? About THEM not the product? Open loops pulling forward? Ends with momentum? Any no → rewrite. (Banned AI tells: see meta-direct-response.md.)

## The Greats, In One Paragraph

Schwartz: match copy length/angle to awareness; less aware = longer. Hopkins: reason-why + specificity + test everything. Ogilvy: 5x read the headline; write like you talk; make truth fascinating. Halbert: starving crowd first; write to one person; stories not pitches. Caples: self-interest beats cleverness; test 5+ headlines. Sugarman: every element's job is the next element; honesty/proof/specificity/familiarity/story. Collier: enter the conversation already in their mind.
