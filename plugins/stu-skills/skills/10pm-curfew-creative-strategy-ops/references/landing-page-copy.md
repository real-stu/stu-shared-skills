# Landing Page / Bridge Page Copy

Short-form bridge and pre-sell pages (300-800 words) that sit between the ad and the VSL/offer. ONE job: get the visitor excited enough to click through. The page does NOT sell, mention price, or reveal a product is for sale.

Adapted from Rob Palmer's landing-page-copy skill (robpalmer.com, CC-BY-4.0).

NOT: a sales page, advertorial, squeeze page, or PDP. Visitor mental state after clicking the ad: "Is this legit? Worth my time? What's this about?" — answer the first two YES, keep the third open enough they must click.

## Classify First

Market/niche; awareness level (most bridge pages target problem-aware cold traffic); destination (VSL/quiz/long page — CTA must match); and the mechanism being teased.

## The Three-Paragraph Framework

**P1 — The Discovery (authority + root cause):** a named expert/institution discovered a specific, novel root cause. Visceral language, named proprietary-sounding mechanism.
> "Researchers at Harvard have detected a toxic blanket that forms around the pancreas — clingy zombie cells that block insulin production."
Authority patterns: "Top Doctor/Scientist", "Researchers at [University]", named expert with geographic specificity.

**P2 — Social Proof (results + benefits):** exact numbers ("over 257,000 individuals", not "thousands"), tangible results, optionally a named testimonial with location. Proof hierarchy (strongest first): named testimonial w/ photo + result + location → verified review screenshots → case study w/ numbers → expert endorsement → media mentions → user counts → anonymous quotes.

**P3 — CTA (urgency + direction):** direct address ("If you or anyone you care about..."), problem restatement, urgency from the *information* not the offer ("before it's taken down", "watch this 3-minute video"), benefit-forward button ("Watch The Free Video", never "Click Here").

## Key Techniques

- **Message match:** the page must feel like a continuation of the ad — same language, mechanism, authority figure. Any disconnect = bounce.
- **Mechanism naming:** [Proprietary Name] + [simple explanation] + [why different] — "The Glucose Flush Method". Test: if a competitor's mechanism name works in your page, yours isn't unique enough.
- **Mechanism tease:** never fully reveal — what it IS (a plant, a method), what it's NOT ("nothing to do with diet, exercise, or medication"), specific hints; resolution lives behind the CTA.
- **"Nothing to do with" pattern:** eliminate their mental models (A) genetics B) diet C) exercise D) medication) — each removes an objection AND raises curiosity.
- **Visceral language:** "toxic blanket" not "cellular buildup"; sensory words — clingy, burning, crushing.
- **60-Second Sales Hook (Rogers)** for story-led pages: Identity → Struggle → Discovery → Result.

## Headline Formulas

Authority+mechanism+benefit ("Top Doctor: Do This To Support Balanced Blood Sugar"); percentage+discovery ("97% Of People Don't Know This..."); mechanism+timeframe ("Simple 7-Second 'Glucose Flush'..."); question; urgency+benefit; story hook.

## Formatting

Mobile first (75%+ of traffic): short paragraphs (2-3 sentences), bold key phrases, big thumb-friendly high-contrast CTA immediately after P3. If it looks like a wall of text on mobile, split or cut.

## Output

Always 2-3 labeled variations testing different angles: Authority Discovery / Social Proof First / Story-Testimonial / Mechanism Mystery / Problem Visceral.

## Voice

Sounds like: a friend urgently sharing a discovery; a breakthrough news report; slightly breathless excitement. Not: a sales pitch, a medical textbook, AI copy (banned tells in meta-direct-response.md). Grade 6 readability. Read aloud — if it sounds like "copy", rewrite.
