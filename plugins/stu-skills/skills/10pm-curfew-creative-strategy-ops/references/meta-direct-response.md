# Meta Direct-Response Ad Copy

Meta-specific (Facebook + Instagram) ad copy mechanics and direct-response strategy: primary text/headline/description craft, awareness-to-funnel routing, Sell-the-Click vs Sell-the-Solution, long video ad formats, and voice anti-patterns. Use for any Meta ad copy task; pairs with hook-writing.md (hooks) and ugc-scriptwriter.md (short UGC).

Adapted from Rob Palmer's ad-copy skill (robpalmer.com, CC-BY-4.0). The ad's ONLY job is to get the click — not close the sale.

## Field Mechanics (rules everything)

| Field | Visible | Max | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary text | ~125 chars | 2,200 | First 125 chars show before "See more" — treat as the headline |
| Headline | ~27 chars | 40 | Below creative; truncates ~27 on mobile; a second hook, never a label ("Learn More" is banned) |
| Description | ~27 chars | 30 | Often hidden on mobile — friction reducers only ("Free shipping", "2-minute read") |

Structure primary text so the first 125 characters create an open loop; the "See more" tap is a micro-conversion. Stories/Reels 9:16, Feed 1:1 or 4:5. 1-2 emojis max; line breaks for white space.

## Classify Before Writing

**Funnel position:** cold (lead with problem/curiosity, longer education) / warm (direct, mechanism-focused) / retargeting (objections, social proof, urgency).

**Awareness level (Schwartz)** — determines length AND destination:

| Level | Lead with | Length | Send to |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Identity/emotion, not the problem | Long, educational | Advertorial |
| Problem-aware | Name the pain vividly, tease mechanism | Medium-long | VSL or advertorial |
| Solution-aware | Differentiate the mechanism | Medium | VSL or long PDP |
| Product-aware | Objections + proof | Short-medium | PDP |
| Most-aware | The deal | Short | Checkout/offer page |

**Strategy — Sell the Click vs Sell the Solution:**
- *Sell the Click*: reveal almost nothing; short caption, curiosity creative; the advertorial/VSL does the selling. Best for unaware/problem-aware, complex or high-ticket.
- *Sell the Solution*: the ad educates, demos, proves; the page just closes. Best for solution-aware+, simple/visual/lower-priced products.
- **Critical rule:** showing the product makes the viewer product-aware — never send product-aware viewers to a blind VSL; send to PDP/offer page. Brands run both strategies simultaneously at different awareness levels.

## WHY / WHAT / HOW Structure (Sultanic)

- **WHY** — open with curiosity/contrarian hook; state and agitate the problem
- **WHAT** — pivot to the mechanism; tease without revealing ("Fortunately there's another way — one that focuses on ___ rather than ___")
- **HOW** — reveal just enough to make the click inevitable

## Intrigue & Mechanism Toolbox

- **Tell them what it's NOT:** "It's not a diet... not a pill... not willpower..."
- **Where it's NOT:** "You can't find it in any store..."
- **Specific hints:** "smaller than a nickel", "grows only above 12,000 feet"
- **Authority anchors:** "A study from [institution] found...", "Over 100 medical reports show..."
- **Strong mechanisms are:** credible, exotic/rare, small and specific ("7-second trick" beats "20-minute routine"), low-effort, expert-backed
- **Bucket brigades (2-3 per ad):** "But here's the thing...", "Wait, it gets better...", "Now here's what nobody tells you..."
- **Proof compression:** exact numbers ("47,312 customers"), named results ("Sarah lost 23 lbs"), timeframes ("in 8 weeks"), visual proof
- **So What? chain:** ask "so what?" of every feature 3 levels deep until emotional/financial; write from the bottom ("close your laptop at 5pm" not "saves 4 hours")
- **Teaching pattern (educational ads):** baseline → unavoidable disruption → their symptoms → stakes if unaddressed. They conclude you must have the solution.

## Long Video Ad Formats

(Short 15-60s UGC → ugc-scriptwriter.md + production-and-ops.md beat maps.)

**ADHD super hook ad (3-7 min in-feed VSL, cold traffic):**
```
[0-30s]     3-5 rapid-fire hooks back-to-back, all open loops, none closed; fast cuts, multiple speakers
[30s-3min]  Teaching section (why the problem exists); tease mechanism; pattern interrupt every 30-45s
[3-5min]    Proof montage: testimonials w/ specific results, before/after, authority mentions
[final 30-60s] Simple low-friction CTA
```
Rules: NEVER name the product (sell the click to VSL/advertorial); never stop hooking; fragmented curiosity, not resolved fascinations; contrarian claims hold attention.

**Story-style video (60-120s, emotional markets):**
```
[0-5s]    Start mid-struggle ("Six months ago I couldn't look at myself in the mirror")
[5-20s]   Failed attempts, relatable frustration
[20-40s]  Discovery/turning point
[40-60s]  Transformation with numbers + timeline
[60-90s]  Natural recommendation, not a pitch ("Just sharing what worked. Link's below.")
```

## Advertorial Matching

Four types the ad may feed: story-based, listicle, editorial/consumer, eCom disruptor (underdog story). Match the ad to the destination — story advertorial → hook teases the story; listicle → tease the #1 finding. Ad + advertorial should feel like one continuous experience.

## Testing & Variations

Every ad request outputs **3-5 labeled variations**, each a different strategic angle (not 5 versions of one opener). Testing hierarchy by impact: 1) hooks (biggest lever — 5-10 per body), 2) images/thumbnails, 3) caption length, 4) video length, 5) copy angle. When a hook wins it becomes the new control.

## Voice & Anti-Patterns

- **Banned AI tells:** delve, dive into, comprehensive, robust, cutting-edge, utilize, leverage, crucial, unlock, unleash, supercharge, game-changer, landscape, navigate, streamline
- **Banned cliches:** "you won't believe", "take your X to the next level", "in today's fast-paced world", "are you ready to"
- **No wimpy hedging:** can/could/should/might → say what it WILL do
- **Structural tells:** uniform sentence length, bullets all starting the same way, prose that sounds written not spoken
- **Tingle factor (Makepeace):** read aloud; wherever it gets boring, unbelievable, or unclear — rewrite there
- **UGC authenticity test:** would a real person say this out loud, on camera, to their phone? If it sounds copywritten, rewrite. Slightly imperfect = believable.
- **Readability:** Flesch-Kincaid grade 6 or lower

## Final Check (before delivering)

1. Does line one stop the scroll? 2. Every claim backed by a specific number/proof? 3. Sounds like talking, not "copy"? 4. Open loops pulling to the click? 5. About THEM, not the product? 6. Rhythm alternates punchy/breathing room? 7. Would a real person say it aloud? 8. CTA benefit-driven, not command-driven? Any no → rewrite that part.

## Shareability (STEPPS)

Social currency (insider/secret positioning), triggers (tie to daily rituals), high-arousal emotion (awe, excitement, anger — not sadness), public (self-advertising messages), practical value, stories (embed the product integrally in a retellable narrative).
